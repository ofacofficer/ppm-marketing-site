The Clifton rental market

Clifton is Passaic County's second-largest city, bordering Paterson, Passaic, Nutley, Bloomfield, and Montclair. Route 3, Route 46, and the Garden State Parkway run through it, and NJ Transit rail plus frequent bus service put Manhattan roughly a half-hour away — so demand comes from commuters and families who want more room than the Hudson County waterfront for the money. The housing stock is a Clifton signature: single-family homes plus a deep supply of two- and three-family houses, garden apartments, and condos across neighborhoods like Allwood, Richfield, Athenia, Botany Village, and Delawanna.

Demand is steady and value-driven. Pricing to the right sub-market, fast turnovers, and reliable maintenance are where Clifton owners protect their return.

Clifton rent regulation

Clifton administers a municipal rent-leveling (rent-control) ordinance, but many one- and two-family and owner-occupied properties are exempt. Whether it applies changes how rent increases work — we confirm your property's status before setting a rent or sending a renewal. Check our Rent Control Lookup, or ask for a straight answer in your free rental analysis.
