The Jersey City rental market

Jersey City is one of the strongest rental markets in New Jersey, driven by its direct PATH train access to Lower Manhattan and Midtown, its diverse neighborhoods, and a sustained influx of NYC-priced-out renters. The Heights, Journal Square, Downtown/Grove, and Greenville all have distinct sub-markets with meaningfully different renter demographics and price points.

Demand is strong across the board, but vacancy rates and achievable rents vary significantly by neighborhood. A 1BR in Downtown Jersey City can command $2,500–$3,200+; the same unit in Greenville or Bergen-Lafayette might rent for $1,400–$1,800. Knowing the micro-market matters.

⚠️ Rent Control Notice

Jersey City has an active rent control ordinance affecting most residential buildings constructed before 1987. Covered properties require registration with the city's Rent Leveling Board, and rent increases are limited to the annual Consumer Price Index (CPI) adjustment. Failure to register or comply can result in fines and the inability to collect increases. PPM handles rent control compliance for all properties we manage.
