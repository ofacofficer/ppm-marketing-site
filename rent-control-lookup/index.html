About NJ Rent Control

New Jersey does not have a statewide rent control law. However, municipalities are permitted to enact their own rent control ordinances. Currently, more than 100 NJ municipalities have active rent control — each with its own caps, exemptions, and administrative requirements.

If your property is in a rent-controlled municipality, you must comply with local rules before increasing rent. Failure to register can eliminate your ability to collect increases for years.
