Property Management Costs & Fees

How Much Does Property Management Cost
in New Jersey? (2025)

A transparent breakdown of what NJ property managers charge — management fees, leasing fees, and the hidden costs that catch landlords off guard.

The short answer

Most NJ property management companies charge between 8% and 12% of monthly collected rent as a management fee, plus a one-time leasing fee when a new tenant is placed.

But the monthly percentage is only part of the picture. Where landlords get surprised is in the additional fees — setup fees, technology fees, maintenance markups, and vacancy fees that some companies layer on top of the base rate. Understanding the full fee structure before you sign a management agreement is essential.

NJ property management fee structures

There are two common fee models in New Jersey:

Percentage of collected rent (most common)

The management company takes a percentage of whatever rent is actually collected each month. This model aligns incentives — if the tenant doesn't pay, the PM doesn't collect their fee either. At 10% management fee:

  • $1,500/month rent → $150/month management fee
  • $2,000/month rent → $200/month management fee
  • $2,500/month rent → $250/month management fee

Watch for "collected vs. due": Some companies charge based on rent due, not rent collected. That means you pay the management fee even when the tenant doesn't pay. Always clarify which model applies.

Flat monthly fee (less common)

A fixed monthly charge regardless of rent amount — typically $100–$200 for single-family homes. Can be advantageous for higher-rent properties.

Is property management worth the cost in NJ?

For most NJ landlords managing more than one or two properties, the math often works out in favor of professional management when you account for:

  • Market rent accuracy: PMs with current market data consistently price at or above what self-managing landlords charge, often recovering more than the management fee in additional rent alone
  • Vacancy reduction: Professional marketing and faster tenant placement reduces costly vacancy periods
  • Legal compliance: A single improper eviction notice, security deposit violation, or rent control error can cost more than years of management fees
  • Time value: Most landlords spend 5–10 hours per unit per month on management tasks

Questions to ask before hiring a NJ property manager

  • Is the fee based on collected rent or rent due?
  • Do you mark up maintenance invoices?
  • Is there a setup fee or technology fee?
  • What is the leasing fee structure?
  • Is there a vacancy fee when the unit is empty?
  • What are the contract termination terms?
